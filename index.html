🌙 About Eid al-Fitr

Understanding the beauty of this blessed celebration

🕌

What is Eid al-Fitr?

Eid al-Fitr, the "Festival of Breaking the Fast," marks the end of Ramadan — a holy month of fasting, prayer, and reflection. It is one of the most joyous celebrations in the Islamic calendar.

🤲

Why We Celebrate

Eid celebrates gratitude to Allah for the strength to complete Ramadan. It is a time of forgiveness, charity (zakat al-fitr), family gatherings, and spreading love to all.

🇮🇩

Lebaran in Indonesia

"Lebaran" is Indonesia's heartfelt name for Eid. It brings the nation together through mudik, festive meals, new clothes, and the beautiful tradition of asking for forgiveness.

🇮🇩 Indonesian Eid Traditions

Beautiful customs that make Lebaran special

🚗

Mudik (Homecoming)

Millions of Indonesians travel to their hometowns to celebrate with family, creating one of the world's largest annual human migrations.

👗

Baju Baru (New Clothes)

Wearing new clothes on Eid symbolizes renewal and fresh beginnings. Families often coordinate matching outfits for Eid morning prayers.

🍽️

Makanan Lebaran (Eid Feast)

Tables overflow with ketupat, opor ayam, rendang, nastar, and kastengels — lovingly prepared dishes that bring families together.

🤝

Halal Bihalal

A uniquely Indonesian tradition of visiting elders and loved ones to ask for forgiveness, strengthening bonds and starting fresh.

Why THR Exists?

Tap to find out!

THR (Tunjangan Hari Raya) is a mandatory holiday bonus in Indonesia — by law, employers must pay it before Eid! It started in the 1950s and became law in 1994.

🔷

Why Ketupat?

Tap to find out!

The diamond-shaped ketupat symbolizes purity and forgiveness. Its woven casing represents life's complexity, and the white rice inside represents a pure heart.

🔄

Eid = Reset Button?

Tap to find out!

Eid feels like a spiritual reset — after 30 days of fasting, self-reflection, and prayer, Muslims emerge renewed, forgiven, and ready for a fresh start!

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Why Families Gather?

Tap to find out!

Eid strengthens silaturahmi — the bond of kinship. In Islam, maintaining family ties is an act of worship, making Eid reunions spiritually meaningful.

🌍

Eid Around the World

Tap to find out!

In Turkey it is called Şeker Bayramı (Sugar Feast), in Malaysia "Hari Raya Aidilfitri," and in Egypt people eat kahk cookies. Each culture adds unique flavors!
